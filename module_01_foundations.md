# Module 01 — Foundations: Understanding AUD/USD

---

## 1.1 What is AUD/USD?

AUD/USD is the currency pair representing the Australian Dollar against the US Dollar.
It tells you how many US dollars you need to buy one Australian dollar.

- **Example:** AUD/USD = 0.6500 means 1 AUD = 0.65 USD
- It is one of the **most liquid pairs** in the world (top 5 by volume)
- It trades approximately **$300+ billion per day**

## 1.3 What Drives AUD/USD?

Understanding the fundamentals gives you **directional bias** before looking at charts.

### Bullish AUD/USD factors:
- Rising commodity prices (gold, iron ore, coal)
- Strong Australian economic data (GDP, employment, CPI)
- RBA (Reserve Bank of Australia) raising interest rates
- Risk-on sentiment globally (AUD is a "risk currency")
- Weak US Dollar (falling DXY)

### Bearish AUD/USD factors:
- Falling commodity prices
- Weak Australian data
- RBA cutting rates or dovish tone
- Risk-off sentiment (investors flee to USD)
- Strong US Dollar (rising DXY / Fed hiking rates)

> 💡 **Key macro rule:** When global risk appetite is HIGH, buy AUD/USD.
> When risk appetite is LOW (recession fears, crises), sell AUD/USD.

## 1.5 Chart Timeframes We Will Use

This course uses a **top-down analysis** approach:

```
Monthly Chart   → Big picture Elliott Wave degree (Grand Supercycle / Supercycle)
Weekly Chart    → Primary Wave count & trend direction
Daily Chart     → Wave sub-structure & trade planning
4-Hour Chart    → Entry timing & MACD divergences
1-Hour Chart    → Fine-tuned entry & stop placement
```

**Golden Rule:** Higher timeframe wave count is the BOSS.
Never trade against the higher timeframe trend unless you have very strong confluence.
