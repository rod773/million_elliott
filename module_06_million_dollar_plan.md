## 6.2 Defining Realistic Performance Targets

Based on this system (Setup A + B on AUD/USD):

**Conservative Assumptions:**
- 4 trades per month (quality setups only)
- Win rate: 45% (below-average, being conservative)
- Average R:R: 1:2.5
- Risk per trade: 1% of account

**Monthly return expectation:**
```
4 trades × 45% win rate = 1.8 wins on average
1.8 wins × 2.5R = +4.5R
2.2 losses × -1R = -2.2R
Net: +2.3R per month
At 1% risk per trade: +2.3% per month
```

**Optimistic Assumptions (after 1+ year experience):**
- 5 trades per month
- Win rate: 55%
- Average R:R: 1:3
- Risk per trade: 1.5%

```
5 trades × 55% = 2.75 wins
2.75 × 3R = +8.25R
2.25 × -1R = -2.25R
Net: +6.0R per month
At 1.5% risk: +9% per month
```

**For this plan, we use the CONSERVATIVE 3% monthly return target.**
This is achievable and leaves room for bad months.

## 6.4 The Scaling Protocol

As your account grows, your risk amount grows automatically with compounding.
BUT your percentage risk per trade stays the same (1–2%).

### Growth Stages and Behavior:

| Stage | Account Size | Lots per Trade (1% risk, 50-pip stop) | Focus |
|-------|-------------|--------------------------------------|-------|
| Learning | $10K–$25K | 0.2–0.5 lots | Strategy mastery |
| Growing | $25K–$100K | 0.5–2.0 lots | Consistency |
| Scaling | $100K–$500K | 2.0–10 lots | Systems & discipline |
| Advanced | $500K–$1M+ | 10–20 lots | Capital preservation |

### Scaling Rule: NEVER increase risk % before proving consistency
Move from 0.5% to 1% risk only after:
- 3 consecutive profitable months
- No monthly drawdown exceeding 4%
- At least 20 completed trades in journal

Move from 1% to 1.5% risk only after:
- 6 consecutive profitable months
- Maximum monthly drawdown: 5%
- Win rate ≥ 45%, R:R ≥ 1:2.5

## 6.5 Drawdown Recovery Protocol

Bad months WILL happen. Here's how to recover:

### If you hit 10% drawdown:
- Reduce risk per trade to 0.5%
- Only trade Setup A and B with maximum confluence (9+ checkboxes)
- Review journal for execution errors
- Continue until account returns to previous high

### If you hit 20% drawdown:
- STOP trading live immediately
- Switch to demo for 30 days minimum
- Identify root cause: strategy error? psychology? market conditions?
- Only return to live after demo performance is restored

### If you hit 30% drawdown:
- Extended break (30–60 days)
- Consider whether the strategy is still working in current market
- Seek an experienced mentor or peer review
- Restart at the smallest possible size ($5K–$10K) on live

## 6.6 When to Take Money Out

Compounding is most powerful when left untouched. But it's YOUR money.

**Suggested Withdrawal Strategy:**
- Months 1–12: No withdrawals — let compounding work
- After $50,000: Withdraw 10% of monthly profits only
- After $100,000: Withdraw 20% of monthly profits
- After $250,000: Withdraw 30% of monthly profits
- After $500,000+: Pay yourself a salary, compound the rest

This way you benefit now AND build toward the million-dollar target.

## 6.7 Broker & Account Considerations

### What to look for in a broker for this strategy:
| Factor | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| Spread on AUD/USD | Under 1.5 pips (ECN/Raw preferred) |
| Execution speed | Fast — not dealing desk |
| Leverage | 30:1 minimum (50:1–100:1 useful) |
| Swap rates | Check — carry cost on overnight trades |
| Account type | ECN/RAW or Standard with tight spreads |
| Regulation | FCA, ASIC, CySEC, or equivalent |

### Leverage Note:
High leverage is a tool, not a goal. We use leverage to allow proper position sizing
with smaller accounts — NOT to take oversized trades. Our risk management keeps exposure controlled.

Once profits become substantial, treat this as a business:
