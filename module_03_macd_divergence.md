# Module 03 — MACD Divergence Mastery

---

## 3.1 What is the MACD?

MACD stands for **Moving Average Convergence Divergence**.
It was developed by Gerald Appel in the late 1970s.

MACD measures the **momentum** and **trend direction** of a market by comparing two
exponential moving averages.

### Standard Settings (used in this course):
- **Fast EMA:** 12 periods
- **Slow EMA:** 26 periods
- **Signal Line:** 9-period EMA of the MACD line

### Components:
| Component | What It Represents |
|-----------|-------------------|
| MACD Line (blue) | 12 EMA minus 26 EMA |
| Signal Line (orange) | 9-period EMA of the MACD line |
| Histogram | MACD Line minus Signal Line |

When MACD Line crosses **above** Signal Line → Bullish signal
When MACD Line crosses **below** Signal Line → Bearish signal

## 3.2 What is Divergence?

Divergence occurs when **price action and MACD momentum disagree**.

This disagreement signals that the current move is losing strength and a
**reversal or deep correction is likely**.

There are two main types:

---

## 3.3 Regular Divergence (Reversal Signal)

### Regular Bearish Divergence
```
Price:  Higher High ↑ (new price high)
MACD:   Lower High  ↓ (lower momentum)
```
Signal: Uptrend is losing momentum → SELL / short opportunity

```
Price Chart:      ...  / Peak1    /Peak2 (higher)
MACD Histogram:   ... / Peak1   / Peak2 (LOWER)
```

**What it means:** Bulls are running out of steam. Each new price high takes MORE
effort and delivers LESS momentum. A reversal is near.

### Regular Bullish Divergence
```
Price:  Lower Low  ↓ (new price low)
MACD:   Higher Low ↑ (higher momentum)
```
Signal: Downtrend is losing momentum → BUY / long opportunity

**What it means:** Bears are running out of steam. Price is making new lows but
momentum is declining — sellers are exhausted.

---

## 3.4 Hidden Divergence (Continuation Signal)

### Hidden Bullish Divergence
```
Price:  Higher Low  ↑ (price pulled back less)
MACD:   Lower Low   ↓ (MACD pulled back more)
```
Signal: Uptrend is healthy → BUY continuation

**What it means:** The pullback in price was shallow (bullish), but MACD dipped more.
This is a trend continuation signal — the uptrend is still intact.

### Hidden Bearish Divergence
```
Price:  Lower High  ↓ (price bounced less)
MACD:   Higher High ↑ (MACD bounced more)
```
Signal: Downtrend is healthy → SELL continuation

**What it means:** The bounce in price was weak (bearish), but MACD bounced more.
The downtrend is still intact.

## 3.5 Divergence Quality Rating

Not all divergences are equal. Rate them before trading:

| Factor | High Quality | Low Quality |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Timeframe | Daily, 4H, Weekly | 15-min, 5-min |
| Gap between peaks/troughs | Wide (many candles) | Narrow (few candles) |
| Elliott Wave position | Wave 5, Wave C end | Random wave |
| Convergence with Fibonacci | Yes | No |
| Price at key S/R level | Yes | No |

**Only trade HIGH quality divergences — 3+ factors confirmed**

## 3.6 How to Draw Divergence Correctly

### Step 1: Identify the swing points on PRICE
- For bearish: find two clear swing highs (price peaks)
- For bullish: find two clear swing lows (price troughs)

### Step 2: Look at the MACD histogram at those same points
- Do the histogram peaks/troughs confirm or deny the price direction?

### Step 3: Draw lines on BOTH
- Draw a line connecting the two price peaks on the price chart
- Draw a line connecting the corresponding MACD histogram peaks
- If one goes up and the other goes down = divergence confirmed

### Common Mistake: Using the wrong MACD component
- Use the **MACD Histogram** for divergence, NOT the MACD line
- The histogram is more sensitive and cleaner for divergence signals

## 3.7 MACD Divergence on Different Timeframes

### Weekly Chart Divergence
- Most powerful signal
- Takes longer to play out (weeks to months)
- Use for major position sizing (Module 06)
- Marks end of Primary degree waves

### Daily Chart Divergence
- Very reliable
- Plays out over days to weeks
- Primary trading signal for this system
- Marks end of Intermediate degree waves

### 4-Hour Chart Divergence
- Good for entry timing
- Plays out over hours to days
- Use to fine-tune entries after Daily divergence signal
- Marks end of Minor degree waves

### 1-Hour Chart Divergence
- Entry-level precision
- Use only when Daily/4H divergence is already present
- Stand-alone 1H divergence is LOW priority

## 3.9 The MACD Zero Line

The MACD zero line is the **equilibrium** between bulls and bears.

**For this system:**
- If MACD crosses above zero = bullish momentum confirmed
- If MACD holds above zero during a pullback = bullish trend intact (hidden divergence zone)
- If MACD crosses below zero = bearish momentum confirmed
- If MACD holds below zero during a bounce = bearish trend intact

**Zero-line bounce rule:**
In a strong Wave 3, the MACD will pull back toward zero but NOT cross below it.
This is a very high-confidence continuation signal.

## 3.10 False Divergence — How to Avoid It

Not every MACD divergence leads to a reversal. Here's how to filter out fakes:

### Red Flags (avoid the trade):
- Divergence on a small timeframe only (no higher TF confirmation)
- Divergence in the MIDDLE of a wave structure (not at Wave 5 or C)
- No key Fibonacci level nearby
- Against a very strong macro trend
- Signal Line hasn't crossed yet (divergence forming but not confirmed)

### Confirmation filters:
1. Wait for the MACD signal line crossover AFTER divergence
2. Look for a candlestick reversal pattern (pin bar, engulfing) at the divergence point
3. Fibonacci confluence (price at key retracement/extension level)
4. Elliott Wave position confirms (Wave 5 or Wave C ending)

## 3.11 Practical MACD Divergence Examples on AUD/USD

### Example Setup 1 — Bullish Divergence at Wave C Low (Buy Setup)
```
Weekly Context: AUD/USD in A-B-C correction after 5-wave rally
Daily Chart: Wave C declining to a 61.8% Fibonacci support
4H Chart: Two swing lows forming — second low LOWER on price, HIGHER on MACD
Signal: Regular Bullish Divergence
Action: BUY at second low, stop below second low, target Wave 3 of new impulse
```

### Example Setup 2 — Bearish Divergence at Wave 5 High (Sell Setup)
```
Weekly Context: AUD/USD in 5-wave impulse up
Daily Chart: Wave 5 approaching 100% extension target of Wave 1
4H Chart: Two swing highs forming — second high HIGHER on price, LOWER on MACD
Signal: Regular Bearish Divergence
Action: SELL at second high, stop above second high, target Wave A of correction
```
