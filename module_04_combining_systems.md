# Module 04 — Combining Elliott Wave + MACD Divergences

---

## 4.1 The Core Philosophy

Neither Elliott Wave nor MACD divergence is perfect on its own.
Elliott Wave can be ambiguous (multiple valid counts).
MACD divergence has false signals.

**Together, they are extremely powerful:**
- Elliott Wave tells you WHERE in the cycle price is
- MACD Divergence tells you WHEN momentum confirms the wave ending
- Combined = High Probability, High Reward Setups

---

## 4.2 The Two Primary Trade Setups

This entire course is built around **two high-probability setups**:

---

### SETUP A: The Wave 5 Reversal Trade

**Logic:** Wave 5 ends the impulse sequence. MACD regularly diverges at Wave 5 tops/bottoms.
The subsequent A-B-C correction is the profit opportunity.

**Conditions Required (ALL must be present):**
1. ✅ Clear 5-wave structure is visible on Daily or 4H chart
2. ✅ Wave 5 has reached a Fibonacci target (61.8%–100% of Wave 1 length)
3. ✅ MACD bearish divergence on the 4H or Daily at the Wave 5 high
4. ✅ Price at a known resistance level (previous high, Fibonacci extension)
5. ✅ Candlestick reversal confirmation (pin bar, bearish engulfing)

**Entry:** At the confirmed reversal candle close (or next candle open)
**Stop Loss:** Above the Wave 5 high (+ spread buffer)
**Target 1:** Wave A = 38.2% retracement of the entire impulse
**Target 2:** Wave C = 61.8% retracement of the entire impulse
**Target 3:** Wave C = 100% retracement back to Wave 1 start level

---

### SETUP B: The Wave 2/C Bottom Entry Trade

**Logic:** After a 5-wave impulse ends and A-B-C correction completes, a NEW 5-wave impulse
begins. Entry at the end of Wave C (or Wave 2) is the safest, highest-reward entry of the cycle.

**Conditions Required (ALL must be present):**
1. ✅ Prior 5-wave structure was clear (impulse completed above/below)
2. ✅ A-B-C correction is complete — Wave C has reached 61.8%–100% of impulse
3. ✅ MACD bullish divergence on the 4H or Daily at the Wave C low
4. ✅ Price at a known support level (previous Wave 4 territory, Fibonacci)
5. ✅ Candlestick reversal confirmation (pin bar, bullish engulfing)

**Entry:** At the confirmed reversal candle close
**Stop Loss:** Below the Wave C low (for long) / Above Wave C high (for short)
**Target 1:** Wave 1 of new impulse = 100% of the corrective wave range
**Target 2:** Wave 3 of new impulse = 161.8% extension of new Wave 1

---

## 4.3 The Step-by-Step Analysis Process

Follow this exact process for EVERY trade:

### Step 1: Weekly Chart — Establish the Macro Wave Count
- What degree are we at? (Cycle? Primary?)
- Are we in an impulse (bullish/bearish) or corrective phase?
- Where is the dominant trend?

### Step 2: Daily Chart — Identify the Current Wave
- Which wave are we currently in?
- Is it Wave 3 (entry missed), Wave 4 (wait for Wave 5), Wave 5 (Setup A brewing), or Wave C (Setup B opportunity)?
- Draw Fibonacci from the most recent impulse start to current high/low

### Step 3: 4H Chart — Look for Divergence
- Is MACD showing divergence at current position?
- Is the divergence at a high-quality position (Wave 5, Wave C)?
- Has the MACD signal line crossed yet?

### Step 4: 1H Chart — Time the Entry
- Find the exact entry candle
- Measure your stop loss distance
- Calculate your position size (Module 05 and 06)

### Step 5: Document the Trade
- Write down your wave count, divergence analysis, Fibonacci levels
- Pre-define your stop loss and targets BEFORE entering
- Note your risk/reward ratio (minimum 1:2, prefer 1:3 or better)

## 4.4 Confluence Checklist

Before entering any trade, check off as many of these as possible:

```
WAVE CONFLUENCE
[ ] Higher timeframe wave count supports direction
[ ] Clear wave structure visible (not ambiguous)
[ ] At a high-probability wave ending position (Wave 5 or Wave C)
[ ] Fibonacci extension/retracement level nearby (within 0.5% of price)
[ ] Alternation pattern between Wave 2 and Wave 4 is respected

MACD CONFLUENCE
[ ] Regular divergence present on 4H or Daily
[ ] MACD signal line has crossed (or is about to)
[ ] Divergence spans at least 20+ candles
[ ] Both price peaks/troughs are clear and obvious

PRICE ACTION CONFLUENCE
[ ] Reversal candlestick at the turning point (pin bar, engulfing)
[ ] Previous S/R level respected
[ ] No major news event in the next 4 hours

MACRO CONFLUENCE (bonus)
[ ] Fundamental backdrop supports direction
[ ] DXY (Dollar Index) aligns with AUD/USD direction
[ ] Commodities (gold) support AUD direction

MINIMUM TO ENTER: 7 out of the above checkboxes
IDEAL ENTRY: 10+ checkboxes
```

## 4.5 Reading the Wave Count — Practical Rules

### Rule: When the wave count is ambiguous, DO NOT trade
There will always be another setup. Forcing an ambiguous count is gambling.
The best trades are obvious. If you're debating between two counts, wait for clarity.

### Rule: Label your alternate count
Always have a "Plan B" wave count. This saves you from being blindsided.

**Example:**
- Primary count: AUD/USD completing Wave 5 up → short Setup A
- Alternate count: Wave 5 is actually Wave 3, and the rally continues
- If price breaks above the Wave 5 high = alternate count activates, exit short

### Rule: Adjust stops to protect against alternate count
Set your stop above/below the level that would invalidate your primary count.

---

## 4.6 The Filter: Quality Over Quantity

**This system should produce 3–6 high-quality trades per month on AUD/USD.**
You do NOT need to trade every day.

Professional traders wait for setup conditions. The discipline to NOT trade is what
separates profitable traders from losing ones.

Quality filter:
- Minimum 1:2 risk-to-reward ratio (prefer 1:3+)
- Only trade when at least 7 confluence factors are present
- Never trade in the last 2 hours before a major news release
- Skip setups if spread is unusually wide (liquidity events)

## 4.7 Setup A in Detail — Wave 5 Sell on AUD/USD

### Scenario: AUD/USD rallying in a 5-wave impulse on the Daily chart

**Wave Count:**
```
Wave 1: 0.6200 → 0.6500 (+300 pips)
Wave 2: 0.6500 → 0.6350 (50% retracement, -150 pips)
Wave 3: 0.6350 → 0.6850 (1.618 × W1 = +484 pips)
Wave 4: 0.6850 → 0.6700 (38.2% of W3, -150 pips)
Wave 5: 0.6700 → 0.6980? (targeting 100% of W1 = +300 pips)
```

**Fibonacci target for Wave 5 high:** 0.6700 + 0.0300 = 0.7000 (round number confluence!)

**On the 4H chart at 0.7000:**
- Price makes a high at 0.6990, then 0.7010 (higher high)
- MACD histogram at 0.6990 peak: 0.0040
- MACD histogram at 0.7010 peak: 0.0025 (LOWER — bearish divergence!)

**Entry Checklist:**
- ✅ Wave 5 at Fibonacci target (0.7000)
- ✅ Round number psychological resistance
- ✅ Bearish divergence on 4H MACD
- ✅ Bearish pin bar / engulfing candle
- ✅ Weekly chart shows primary uptrend nearly complete

**Trade:**
- Entry: 0.7005 (after bearish engulfing close)
- Stop: 0.7050 (above Wave 5 high + buffer = 45 pips)
- Target 1: 0.6700 (Wave A = 38.2% retracement = 305 pips)
- Target 2: 0.6450 (Wave C = 61.8% retracement = 555 pips)
- R:R on Target 1: 305/45 = **6.8:1**
- R:R on Target 2: 555/45 = **12.3:1**

## 4.8 Setup B in Detail — Wave C Bottom Buy on AUD/USD

### Scenario: AUD/USD completing A-B-C correction on Daily after 5-wave decline

**Wave Count (corrective rally opportunity):**
```
Prior 5-wave decline: 0.7500 → 0.6200 (-1300 pips)
Wave A correction up: 0.6200 → 0.6600 (+400 pips)
Wave B down: 0.6600 → 0.6350 (-250 pips)
Wave C up: 0.6350 → ??? (target = Wave A = +400 pips → 0.6750)
```

At 0.6750:
- Price reaches 61.8% retracement of the full 5-wave decline
- Wave C = Wave A in length
- 4H MACD: bullish divergence (higher lows on MACD while Wave C makes slight new high)
- Candlestick: bullish pin bar

Wait — this is the SELL not buy. Let me illustrate correctly:

**After a 5-wave decline and A-B-C correction, BUY new impulse:**
```
5-wave decline completed at 0.6200
A-B-C correction upward (counter-trend) completed at 0.6700 (61.8% of decline)
New 5-wave impulse DOWN beginning from 0.6700
Wave 1 down: 0.6700 → 0.6400 (-300 pips)
Wave 2 up: 0.6400 → 0.6550 (50% retrace, +150 pips)
Now watching for Wave 2 to end and Wave 3 down to begin
```

**Setup B Entry (short):**
- Wave 2 at 0.6550 (50% Fibonacci of Wave 1)
- 4H MACD: bearish divergence forming at 0.6550
- Entry: 0.6540 short
- Stop: 0.6620 (above Wave 2 high = 80 pips)
- Target Wave 3: 0.6400 × 1.618 = 0.6014 (~526 pips)
- R:R: 526/80 = **6.6:1**

## 4.9 Module 04 Summary

- Use Elliott Wave to know WHERE you are in the cycle
- Use MACD Divergence to know WHEN the wave is ending
- Setup A: Wave 5 reversal — trade the end of an impulse
- Setup B: Wave C/2 completion — trade the start of a new impulse
- Follow the 5-step analysis process for every single trade
- Quality over quantity — 3–6 trades/month maximum
