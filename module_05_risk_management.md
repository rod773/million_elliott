# Module 05 — Trade Management & Risk Framework

---

## 5.1 Why Risk Management is More Important Than the Strategy

You can have the best trading strategy in the world and still lose everything.

The reason: **poor risk management**.

Conversely, a mediocre strategy WITH excellent risk management can be consistently profitable.

**The goal is NOT to win every trade. The goal is to survive long enough to let your edge compound.**

---

## 5.2 The Foundation: Risk Per Trade

### The 1–2% Rule
Never risk more than 1–2% of your total trading capital on any single trade.

| Account Size | 1% Risk | 2% Risk |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| $10,000 | $100 | $200 |
| $25,000 | $250 | $500 |
| $50,000 | $500 | $1,000 |
| $100,000 | $1,000 | $2,000 |
| $250,000 | $2,500 | $5,000 |

**For beginners:** Start with 0.5% risk per trade while learning
**For experienced traders:** 1% is conservative and sustainable
**Maximum:** Never exceed 2% unless you have a proven 1+ year track record

> Why? A 10-trade losing streak (which CAN happen) at 2% risk = ~18% drawdown.
> At 1% risk = ~10% drawdown. You can recover from 10%.
> A 50% drawdown requires a 100% gain just to break even.

## 5.3 Calculating Position Size

### Formula:
```
Position Size (lots) = (Account Size × Risk %) / (Stop Loss in pips × Pip Value)
```

### Pip Value for AUD/USD:
- 1 Standard Lot (100,000 units) = $10 per pip
- 1 Mini Lot (10,000 units) = $1 per pip
- 1 Micro Lot (1,000 units) = $0.10 per pip

### Example Calculation:
```
Account Size:  $50,000
Risk Per Trade: 1% = $500
Stop Loss:      50 pips
Pip Value:      $10/pip (Standard Lot)

Position Size = $500 / (50 × $10) = $500 / $500 = 1.0 Standard Lot
```

### Another Example:
```
Account Size:   $10,000
Risk Per Trade: 1% = $100
Stop Loss:      40 pips

Position Size = $100 / (40 × $10) = 0.25 Standard Lots = 2.5 Mini Lots
```

**NEVER size your position first then set your stop.
ALWAYS define the stop first, THEN calculate position size.**

## 5.4 Stop Loss Placement

### Rule: Stops go at WAVE INVALIDATION POINTS

Place your stop where your wave count is proven WRONG:
- Setup A (Wave 5 sell): Stop above Wave 5 high + buffer
- Setup B (Wave 2 short): Stop above Wave 2 high + buffer
- Long entries: Stop below corrective wave low

### Buffer Rule:
Add 5–10 pips beyond the invalidation level to account for spread and wicks.

### ATR-Based Stops (Alternative):
Use the 14-period ATR on your entry timeframe.
Stop = Invalidation level + 0.5 × ATR

## 5.5 Take Profit Strategy — Scaling Out

Never exit your entire position at one target. Scale out in portions:

### Standard Scaling Method:
```
Position: 1.0 Standard Lot

Target 1 (Wave A / 38.2% retrace):   Close 40% → move remaining stop to breakeven
Target 2 (Wave C start / 61.8%):     Close 40% → trail stop by wave structure
Target 3 (Full correction complete): Close final 20% → maximum profit, minimum risk
```

**Why scale out?**
- Locks in profit early
- Lets a portion run for maximum reward
- Removes emotional pressure from the trade

---

## 5.6 Breakeven Rule

Once the trade reaches 1:1 R:R (profit equals initial risk), move stop to breakeven.

**Example:**
- Short entry: 0.7005, Stop: 0.7050 (45 pips)
- 1:1 level: 0.6960 (45 pips profit)
- Once price hits 0.6960 → move stop to 0.7005
- Trade is now risk-free on remaining position

---

## 5.7 Trailing Stop Strategy

### Wave-Based Trailing:
- Trail stop above each completed minor corrective wave high (for shorts)
- After Wave A confirmed → trail above Wave B high
- After Wave B confirmed → trail using Wave B retracement levels

### Price Action Trailing:
- After each 4H candle closes below a prior swing low (shorts)
- Trail by 50% of the ATR on the 4H chart

---

## 5.8 Maximum Daily Loss Limit

**Hard Rule: Stop trading if you lose 3% of your account in a single day.**

When you hit 3%:
1. Close all positions
2. Step away from screens
3. Review what happened
4. Return the next day

Also stop trading after 3 consecutive losing trades regardless of dollar amount.
Your mindset is compromised after consecutive losses.

---

## 5.9 Maximum Monthly Drawdown

**Set a monthly maximum drawdown of 6–8% of account.**

If hit, stop trading for the rest of that month and review your setups.
Ask: Was this bad luck or bad execution?

---

## 5.10 The Risk/Reward Requirement

**Minimum acceptable R:R for this system: 1:2**
**Target R:R: 1:3 to 1:6**

Why R:R matters more than win rate:

```
Win Rate 40%, R:R 1:2:
10 trades: 4 wins × $200 + 6 losses × -$100 = $800 - $600 = +$200 PROFIT

Win Rate 40%, R:R 1:1:
10 trades: 4 wins × $100 + 6 losses × -$100 = $400 - $600 = -$200 LOSS
```

With R:R of 1:3 you need only a 25% win rate to break even.

## 5.11 News Events — The Non-Negotiable Rule

**NEVER hold through high-impact news without a plan.**

Key AUD/USD news events:
- RBA Interest Rate Decision (first Tuesday monthly)
- Australian CPI
- Australian Employment Data
- US Non-Farm Payrolls (first Friday monthly)
- US FOMC Rate Decision
- US CPI

**Options before news:**
1. Close the position entirely (safest)
2. Reduce position size by 50%
3. Widen stop to account for spike (recalculate risk)

Use Forex Factory or DailyFX economic calendar.

## 5.13 Module 05 Summary

- Max 1–2% risk per trade; start at 0.5% while learning
- Always set stop first, then calculate position size
- Stop at wave invalidation level + buffer
- Scale out: 40% at T1, 40% at T2, 20% at T3
- Move stop to breakeven at 1:1 R:R
- Hard limits: 3% daily max, 8% monthly max drawdown
- Minimum 1:2 R:R on every trade
- Plan around all high-impact news events
- Journal every trade — no exceptions
